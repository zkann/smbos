# Ask a customer for a review or testimonial

## Purpose

Convert happy moments into public proof (reviews, testimonials) with a personal ask at the right time, instead of never asking.

## When to use

Right after a happy moment: project delivered well, problem solved, repeat purchase, unprompted praise. NOT during an open support issue or a rough patch.

## Inputs

- The customer and the specific happy moment to reference
- The destination link [personalize: which platform matters most: Google, Yelp, G2, your testimonials page?]

## Steps

1. Confirm the timing is right: no open issues with this customer, and the win is recent.
2. Draft a short personal ask: reference the specific moment, one direct link, and make "no" easy.
3. If asking for a testimonial quote (vs a platform review), offer to draft a starting point from what they already said, for them to edit.
4. **[APPROVAL]** Owner reviews; these are personal messages, not campaigns.
5. Send, and log who was asked and when so nobody gets asked twice in a year.
6. When a review lands: thank them personally, and save the quote to the proof file. [personalize: where do testimonials/quotes get collected?]

## My way

- [personalize: do you offer any thank-you or incentive? Note: review platforms often prohibit incentivized reviews; default is none.]
- One ask, one polite nudge two weeks later if no response, then drop it.
- Never ask a customer to change or remove a critical review.

## Edge cases

- Customer replies with criticism instead of a review: thank them, treat it as support feedback, and do not resend the review link.
