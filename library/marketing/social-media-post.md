# Write and schedule a social media post

## Purpose

Turn an idea, a win, or a piece of content into platform-appropriate posts in the owner's voice, on a sane schedule.

## When to use

Routine posting on the business's channels. NOT for responding to controversy or anything time-sensitive and risky; the owner handles those personally.

## Inputs

- The seed: an idea, a blog post, a customer win, a lesson learned
- Which platforms this should go to [personalize: which platforms do you actually use?]

## Steps

1. Draft a separate variant per platform. No cross-posting the same text everywhere; length, format, and tone differ.
2. Each post: a hook in the first line, one idea, plain language.
3. **[APPROVAL]** Owner reviews every post before it is scheduled. Social posts are public and permanent.
4. Schedule at the standard times. [personalize: scheduling tool and preferred times?]
5. Next day, check replies and surface any that need the owner.

- Topics that are off-limits: [personalize: politics? competitors? client names?]
- Never post about a client without permission, even praise.
- No engagement-bait formats ("Agree?", rage hooks, fake polls).

## Edge cases

- A post starts getting negative traction: do not reply or delete on autopilot; flag the owner with a screenshot and wait.
