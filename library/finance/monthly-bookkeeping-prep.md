# Monthly bookkeeping prep

## Purpose

Get the month's transactions categorized and reconciled so the books (or the accountant) start from clean data.

## When to use

First week of each month, for the prior month. NOT a substitute for the accountant's actual close or tax work.

## Inputs

- Bank and credit card transaction exports for the month [personalize: which accounts, and where do exports come from?]
- Access to the bookkeeping tool [personalize: QuickBooks, Xero, a spreadsheet?]

## Steps

1. Export all transactions for the month.
2. Categorize each against the standard category list. [personalize: paste or link your category list]
3. Collect everything uncategorizable into one list with dates and amounts. Do not guess categories.
4. **[APPROVAL]** Walk the owner through the unknowns and any unusually large or duplicate-looking charges.
5. Reconcile category totals against the account statement totals; investigate any mismatch.
6. Write a five-line summary: revenue, top three expense categories, anything anomalous.
7. File or send. [personalize: does this go to an accountant? Who, and in what format?]

## My way

- [personalize: recurring transactions that always confuse categorization, and where they belong]
- Flag any new subscription not seen in prior months; subscription creep is a standing concern.
- Never delete or merge transactions to force a reconciliation; surface the mismatch.

## Edge cases

- Personal expense on the business card: separate list, marked for owner draw or reimbursement, never categorized as a business expense.
