# Meeting follow-up and recap

## Purpose

Turn a meeting into a written record of decisions and owned action items, sent while everyone still remembers the conversation.

## When to use

After any client or partner meeting with decisions or commitments in it. Internal solo notes do not need this.

## Inputs

- Meeting notes, transcript, or the owner's verbal download
- Attendee list

## Steps

1. Extract from the notes: decisions made, action items (each with an owner and a date), open questions.
2. Anything ambiguous, ask the owner now rather than guessing what was agreed.
3. Draft the recap email: two or three sentences of context, then decisions, then action items grouped by owner.
4. **[APPROVAL]** Owner reviews before it goes to a client.
5. Send to all attendees.
6. Put OUR action items where tasks live. [personalize: which task system?]
7. Set follow-up checks for THEIR action items due dates.

## My way

- Send same day as the meeting. [personalize: or is next morning fine?]
- Action items are one line each: verb, owner, date. No paragraphs.
- [personalize: tone with clients: first names? formal?]
- Never put anything in a recap that was not actually said in the meeting; recaps are records, not negotiations.

## Edge cases

- The meeting included a disagreement: state the open question neutrally, do not editorialize either side.
