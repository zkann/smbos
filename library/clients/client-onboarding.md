# Onboard a new client

## Purpose

Take a client from "signed" to "work underway" with nothing dropped, so the first week sets the tone for the engagement.

## When to use

Contract signed (or deposit paid) for a new client or a returning client starting a new engagement.

## Inputs

- Signed agreement and confirmation of any deposit [personalize: do you require a deposit before kickoff?]
- Client contact(s), and who the decision maker is
- Scope summary from the proposal

## Steps

1. Verify the agreement is signed and the deposit (if required) is received. Do not start onboarding on a promise.
2. Send the welcome email: what happens next, who they will hear from, and what you need from them. [personalize: paste or link your welcome email template, if any]
3. Request access and assets via a checklist. [personalize: what do you typically need? Accounts, brand assets, credentials, points of contact?]
4. Set up the internal side: project space, folder, billing record. [personalize: which tools?]
5. **[APPROVAL]** Propose 2 or 3 kickoff call slots before sending the scheduling email.
6. Schedule the kickoff and confirm the agenda.
7. After kickoff, send a recap with decisions and immediate next steps (see the meeting follow-up SOP if installed).

## My way

- [personalize: anything you always do personally rather than delegate to the AI?]
- Credentials go through a password manager or the client's own access grant. Never accept passwords over email without flagging it.
- One consolidated asks-list, not a dribble of requests.

## Edge cases

- Client goes quiet during onboarding: one nudge per week, and flag to the owner after two weeks of silence.
