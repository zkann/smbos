# Reply to a customer support message

## Purpose

Answer customers quickly and consistently, resolve what can be resolved, and escalate what the owner needs to see.

## When to use

Any inbound customer question, complaint, bug report, or refund request.

## Inputs

- The full thread, not just the latest message
- The customer's account, order, or subscription status [personalize: where do you look this up?]

## Steps

1. Read the whole thread and look up the customer's history before drafting anything.
2. Classify: question, bug report, refund request, or upset customer. More than one can apply.
3. Draft the reply: answer the actual question first, then any housekeeping.
4. Refund requests: apply the policy below. Within policy, approve in the draft; outside policy, escalate.
5. **[APPROVAL]** Owner reviews replies to upset customers, refunds outside policy, and anything with legal or public-review risk. Routine answers can send directly once this SOP is trusted.
6. Send, and log the issue if it is a bug or recurring theme. [personalize: where do bugs/themes get logged?]

## My way

- Refund policy: [personalize: what do you approve without thinking, and where is the line?]
- Tone: [personalize: warm and casual, or brief and professional?]
- Acknowledge within one business day even if the fix takes longer.
- Never blame the customer, even when it is user error; explain the fix instead.

## Edge cases

- Threat of chargeback or public review: stop, escalate to the owner immediately, no reply without approval.
- Angry message at night: do not send a reply composed in the moment; queue it for morning review.
