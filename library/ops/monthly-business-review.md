# Monthly business review

## Purpose

A one-page look back at the month and a short list of priorities for the next one. The zoom-out the weekly report is too noisy for.

## When to use

First few days of each month. NOT a board deck or investor update.

## Inputs

- The month's weekly metrics reports, if they exist
- Revenue and expense totals for the month
- The prior monthly review (for last month's stated priorities)

## Steps

1. Pull monthly totals: revenue, expenses, profit, plus the two or three operating metrics that matter. [personalize: which operating metrics?]
2. Compare against the prior month and the same month last year if data exists.
3. Score last month's priorities: done, partial, or dropped, with one line each. Honest scoring; "dropped" is information.
4. Draft wins (top 3), problems (top 3), and proposed priorities for next month (max 3).
5. **[APPROVAL]** Review with the owner; the priorities list is theirs to set, not the AI's.
6. Save to the same place every month. [personalize: where?]

## My way

- One page maximum. If it does not fit, cut problems commentary before cutting numbers.
- Priorities are concrete actions, not themes. "Raise prices on plan X" not "improve revenue".
- [personalize: anything you always want tracked month over month, e.g. cash on hand, runway?]

## Edge cases

- A one-time event distorts the month (big annual payment, refund spike): show totals with and without it, labeled.
