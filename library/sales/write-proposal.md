# Write a proposal

## Purpose

Turn a qualified conversation into a written proposal the prospect can say yes to, in the owner's standard structure and pricing style.

## When to use

After a discovery conversation, when the prospect has a real problem, a budget signal, and a decision maker identified. NOT as a substitute for discovery.

## Inputs

- Discovery notes: their problem, success criteria, timeline, budget signals
- Which past proposal is the closest match [personalize: where do past proposals live?]

## Steps

1. Confirm the inputs exist. If there was no discovery conversation, say so; a proposal without discovery is a guess.
2. Draft: problem statement in THEIR words, proposed approach, scope (what is included AND what is not), timeline, pricing, next step.
3. Price it per the pricing style below.
4. **[APPROVAL]** Owner reviews scope and pricing. Always.
5. Send with an expiration date and a single clear next step.
6. Log it in the pipeline and schedule a follow-up. [personalize: where is the pipeline tracked? How many days until follow-up?]

## My way

- Pricing style: [personalize: single price, or 2-3 options? Fixed fee, retainer, or hourly?]
- Proposal validity: [personalize: how long until the quote expires?]
- The "not included" list is mandatory; scope creep starts in vague proposals.
- Never discount in the written proposal without the owner deciding it first.

## Edge cases

- Prospect asks for a price before discovery: give a range and the conditions that move it, not a number.
